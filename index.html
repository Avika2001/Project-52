Fun Facts about Camels:

  • The 2 types of camel are one humped dromedary camels and two humped bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants.

Fun Facts about Chameleons:

  • Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads.
  • There are around 160 species of chameleon.
  • When a chameleon is cold, it will become darker in color.

Fun Facts about Cicadas:

  • Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans.
  • There are around 3,000 cicada species.
  • Cicadas live on all continents except Antarctica.

Fun Facts about Elephants:

  • Elephants are the world's largest land animal.
  • You can tell two species apart by their ears.
  • Elephant calves are able to stand within 20 minutes.

Fun Facts about Pandas:

  • Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo.
  • Baby pandas are born pink and measure about 15cm.
  • Unlike most other bears, pandas do not hibernate.

Fun Facts about Pigeons:

  • Pigeons can recognise each letter of the human alphabet.
  • Pigeons will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet.

Fun Facts about Teddy Bears:

  • Teddy bears have babylike features to enhance their cuteness.
  • Teddy bears were named for U.S. President Theodore Roosevelt.
  • The smallest teddy bear is 0.29 inches tall.

Fun Facts about Foxes:

  • Foxes can make around 40 different noises.
  • A group of foxes is called a skulk or leash.
  • Foxes whiskers help them to navigate.
